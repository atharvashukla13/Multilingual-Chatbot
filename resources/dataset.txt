Ayurvedic Q&A & Benchmark Data
BhashaBench-Ayur (BBA) – A bilingual Ayurvedic QA benchmark (English + Hindi) of ~15,000
authentic exam questions (MCQs, fill-blanks, reasoning) covering 15+ Ayurvedic domains . The
dataset (Hugging Face) draws on government and institutional exams (UPSC AYUSH, AIAPGET, etc.).
It includes 9,348 English and 5,615 Hindi questions . BBA is available via Hugging Face (cc-by-4.0
license) .
Conversational Medical Dialogues
MediTOD – Doctor–patient dialogue dataset for medical history-taking (English). Collected and
annotated with comprehensive medical slot labels . It contains simulated clinical interviews
(focused on respiratory cases) with patient histories and symptom mentions. MediTOD is publicly
released (GitHub) for research .
Structured Symptom-Disease Data
Hindi-English Health Cases (Zenodo) – A multilingual medical dataset with clinical case records.
Includes English_dataset.csv and Hindi_dataset.csv, with patient demographics, diagnoses,
symptoms, treatments and durations . (Also a Punjabi version.) Covers many conditions,
symptoms and their Ayurvedic-related treatments. Licensed CC0 (public domain) .
Disease–Symptom Dataset (2023) – Tabular English dataset with 773 diseases and 377 symptoms
(~246k rows, synthetically generated) . Each row links one or more symptoms to a disease (with
symptom severity and disease prevalence info). Released on Mendeley Data under CC-BY-4.0 .
Useful for symptom-based retrieval or classification tasks.
Ayurvedic Text Corpora
Charaka Samhita (English) – Complete English translation of the Charaka Samhita (1949) freely
available online. Covers core Ayurvedic principles, pathology, anatomy, therapeutics, pharmacology,
etc. . (WisdomLib hosts the full text; e.g. Sutra Sthana, Nidana Sthana, etc.)
Sushruta Samhita (English) – Volume 1 (Sūtrasthāna) English translation (1907) is openly accessible.
It contains an extensive summary of Ayurvedic medicine (including surgical techniques, diagnostics,
regimens) . (WisdomLib provides the full Sūtrasthāna text online.)
(Other classical Ayurvedic works have similar online editions – e.g. parts of Ashtanga Hridaya – which can be used
as corpora for language models.)

Multilingual Health Corpora
AI4Bharat IN22-Gen – General-domain parallel benchmark in 22 Indic languages (including Hindi)
with English as source . Contains 1,024 sentence pairs (covering domains like health, law, news,
etc.) in 22 languages. For Hindi-English ( hin_Deva – eng_Latn ), this can serve as training/
evaluation data for translation/understanding of health-related text. Licensed CC-BY-4.0 .
IIT Bombay English–Hindi Parallel Corpus – A large general-domain parallel dataset (news, tech,
health, etc.) covering multiple domains in English and Hindi. Available for research (CC BY-NC-4.0). It
includes some health-related material (and can supplement domain-specific data).
Indic Health Translations – (Examples) The Zenodo and IN22 datasets above include aligned Hindi-
English content. Other resources like the AI4Bharat Indic translation corpus and Wikimedia
translations also provide health-related sentences (often CC-BY).
Additional Resources
Medical QA in Hindi (maternal/child health) – ASHA-FAQ – A vetted Hindi QA corpus for maternal/
child health (over 1,300 Q/A pairs) developed by SCARL (details in Yadav et al. 2023). (The full dataset
is not yet openly published, but an example demo is on GitHub.)
General Hindi Q&A (TELUS) – A Hindi-language QA corpus (1,000+ questions) for general domain.
(Commercially licensed; mentioned for reference, not open.)
Each dataset above is publicly accessible for download (with the noted license). For example, Zenodo
provides direct CSV downloads , Hugging Face hosts BhashaBench-Ayur (cc-by) with an API to load
, and WisdomLib sites allow copying classical Ayurvedic texts for research .
